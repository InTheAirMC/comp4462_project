The Academy's Blind Spot

For decades, the Oscars have celebrated a very specific type of film: period dramas, tragic biopics, and stories about Hollywood itself. These "Oscar bait" movies follow a predictable formula — serious subject matter, transformative makeup, and a soaring orchestral score.

But here's the problem: audiences are tired of it. While critics praise these technically flawless films, regular moviegoers are checking their watches.

"The more a film tries to win an Oscar, the less it tries to win an audience."
📊 THE DATA

The Widening Gap

Audience scores are plummeting. Since 2010, Rotten Tomatoes audience ratings for Best Picture nominees have dropped by nearly 28%, while the number of nominations for "prestige" films has barely changed.

This scatter plot tells the story: each dot represents a nominee. Notice how the cluster moves leftward (lower audience scores) while staying high on the nomination axis.

⭐ The conclusion? The Academy is out of touch with what people actually enjoy watching.
